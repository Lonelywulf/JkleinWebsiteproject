The Bullwhip Effect

You might find yourself asking, what is the bullwhip effect? It's a phenomenon that describes the supply chain issues at each point. It begins when customers engage in unpredictable shopping habits. Retailers react by saturating the errant demand through larger orders.

These larger orders increase stress on distributors, who often react the same way: increasing order size. The final chain is the manufacturer, attempting to manage the over-ordering.

You can visualize the bullwhip effect like a person cracking a whip. The ripples are small near the person but huge at the end of the whip. If the customer is the person, the retailer is the whip's handle. The distributor is in the middle of the whip, with the manufacturer being the tail of the whip. 

How Do You Insulate Your Business?

Retailers have used demand signals from shopping habits to focus their orders in the past. World events have created mass distortions in demand yield forecasts. Inefficient supply chains and low inventory are typical due to these outdated forecasts.

The pandemic distortions have made forecasting demand yield impossible. These signals are no longer accurate. Retailers have maintained their traditional signal forecasting, growing their orders by magnitudes. But these efforts continue to create the bullwhip effect.

With many large retailers operating in a reactive mode, the disorders have become systemic. The resultant static forecasts are inaccurate and ineffective at predicting demand and satiating supply. 

So how do you insulate yourself and your business from these adverse effects? It has become impossible for standard methods to predict demand. Minimizing the bullwhip effect is critical. You need a new paradigm in demand signal forecasting to assess the situation accurately.
